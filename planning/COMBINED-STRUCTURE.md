## FONT RECOMMENDATIONS (Dyslexia/Accessibility Friendly)

Google Sites offers limited font choices through its theme settings. Here are the best options for accessibility:

### Option A (Recommended): Lexend + Open Sans
- **Headings:** Lexend — specifically designed for reading proficiency, excellent for dyslexia
- **Body:** Open Sans — clean, wide letter spacing, highly legible
- **Why:** Lexend was created through research at Bonnie Shaver-Troup's lab specifically to reduce visual stress. Open Sans has open apertures and neutral letterforms.

### Option B: Roboto + Roboto
- **Headings:** Roboto (Bold/Medium)
- **Body:** Roboto (Regular/Light)
- **Why:** Excellent letter distinction (b/d, p/q don't mirror). Wide availability in Google Sites.

### Option C: Lato + Lato (current BBC site uses this)
- **Headings:** Lato Bold
- **Body:** Lato Regular
- **Why:** Good readability, semi-rounded letterforms, already in use

### General Accessibility Rules for the Site:
- Minimum body text size: 16px (Google Sites default)
- Line spacing: 1.5x minimum
- High contrast: dark text on light background
- Avoid italics for long passages
- Left-align text (not justified)
- Short paragraphs, generous white space
- Avoid ALL CAPS for body text (OK sparingly for headings)
