## Executive Summary

The FASD United Mental Health Work Group is requesting support from FASD United to expand the brain-based mental health initiative through the upcoming grant cycle. This proposal outlines who we are, the purpose of the Work Group, and what the grant funding will be used for: continued website development, the Brain-Based Pathway learning content (including mini training videos), the Community of Practice for clinicians, CEUs through FASD United, and translation of evolving research into accessible practice resources.

## About the Work Group

The FASD United Mental Health Work Group is a collaborative of mental health clinicians, caregivers, self-advocates, and researchers who recognized a shared gap: too few people in the field truly understood individuals with brain-based differences, and the standard mental health training pathway did not prepare clinicians to work with those clients well.

The Work Group came together as a community to close that gap through education, training, and a shared commitment to making brain-based care more accessible to everyone who needs it.

## Purpose

The Work Group exists to:

1. **Close the brain-based mental health gap.** Build accessible, accurate, evidence-informed resources that help mental health clinicians, caregivers, and self-advocates understand brain-based differences and respond well to them.

2. **Develop the Brain-Based Pathway.** A structured developmental framework that supports clinicians from foundational brain-based understanding through to advanced, responsive practice — across five core steps.

3. **Establish a clinician Community of Practice.** A peer-learning space for clinicians working with FASD and other brain-based differences, supporting ongoing professional development beyond what graduate training typically provides.

4. **Bridge research and practice.** Translate evolving research (including FASD, neurodevelopment, trauma, and neurodiversity-affirming care) into resources clinicians can actually use, while being honest about what comes from current clinical practice vs. emerging research.

5. **Expand reach over time.** Develop parallel pathways for caregivers and self-advocates so the framework speaks to all three audiences, not only clinicians.

## What the Grant Will Be Used For

Five focus areas, each tied to a deliverable.

### 1. Website and Educational Content
- Continued development and hosting of the brain-based mental health resource site (`tcfasd.github.io/Brain-Based-Mental-Health-/`)
- Editing, revisions, and accessibility improvements
- Site maintenance and updates as evidence and resources evolve

### 2. Mini Training Videos for the Brain-Based Pathway
- Development and production of 10-minute training videos for each step in the Brain-Based Pathway:
  - Step 1 — Brain-Based Foundations
  - Step 2 — Assessments through a Brain-Based Lens
  - Step 3 — Treatment Planning with the Brain in Mind
  - Step 4 — Adapting Your Approach
  - Step 5 — Reflective Supervision and Community
- Each video covers core content for the step, designed for clinicians to consume in short, manageable segments

### 3. Community of Practice for Clinicians
- Launch and ongoing facilitation of the Community of Practice
- Coordination with institutional partners (FASD United, University of Alberta, and others as they join)
- Resources and infrastructure for peer consultation, case discussion, and shared learning
- Outreach to grow membership across the FASD field

### 4. CEUs Through FASD United
- Establishment of CEU pathways for clinicians completing the Brain-Based Pathway content
- Partnership with FASD United for accreditation and ongoing CEU administration
- Documentation and tracking infrastructure

### 5. Research Translation
- Incorporating evolving research into the site as it develops
- Connection to FASD research efforts and integration of new findings into the Brain-Based Pathway
- Educational navigation support for clinicians keeping up with the evolving field

## Long-term Vision

The Mental Health Work Group aims to be a central resource for clinicians, caregivers, and self-advocates navigating brain-based mental health care, with FASD as the anchoring focus and broader applicability to all neurodevelopmental and brain-based differences. Beyond the current clinician-focused work, the long-term vision includes:

- Parallel Brain-Based Pathways for caregivers and self-advocates
- Expanded Community of Practice with additional institutional partners
- A research-to-practice pipeline that translates emerging FASD and neurodevelopmental research into accessible clinical resources
- Sustained partnership with FASD United as the institutional home
