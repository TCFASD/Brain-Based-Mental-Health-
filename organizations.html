For Organizations

Building brain-based capacity in your team

Is Your Team Equipped?

Are you an agency leader, clinical director, or supervisor trying to better support your team when they're working with complex, neurodevelopmental presentations?

Most graduate programs and clinical training assume typical brain functioning. Brain-based practice doesn't replace what your team already does — it helps them understand why certain clients aren't responding, and what to do differently.

What a Brain-Based Lens Gives Your Clinical Team

Understanding

A framework for actually understanding complex neurodivergence — not just managing it. Strategies that work with actual brain capacity, not against it.

Reduced Burnout

Reduced blame of clients, families, and themselves. Better outcomes and lower staff burnout when the approach matches the neurology.

Confidence

Confidence working with populations others avoid. Your team becomes the referral destination, not the one making the referral.

Who Needs Brain-Based Training?

Brain-based care is systemic. When only therapists understand brain-based approaches but intake staff, case managers, and supervisors don't, the system breaks down.

Clinical Team

  • Therapists and counselors — Direct service providers need the deepest training across all six competency stages
  • Clinical supervisors — Must understand the framework to support clinicians in applying it
  • Assessment/diagnostic staff — Need brain-based assessment skills to identify presentations accurately

Broader Team

  • Case managers and care coordinators — Need to understand brain-based accommodations and communicate effectively
  • Intake and administrative staff — First point of contact; understanding brain-based communication reduces barriers
  • Psychiatry/prescribers — Medication management looks different with FASD and neurodevelopmental differences

Sample Onboarding Plan

1

Build Shared Understanding and Language

Establish a common foundation across your team so everyone — from intake to clinical — speaks the same brain-based language.

2

Translate Knowledge into Practice

Move from understanding brain-based differences to applying that understanding in assessments, treatment plans, and daily interactions.

3

Integrate Brain-Based Practices

Embed brain-based thinking into your organization's workflows, supervision structures, and quality metrics.

4

Continuing Education

Sustain growth through ongoing training, reflective supervision, and connection to the broader brain-based community.
