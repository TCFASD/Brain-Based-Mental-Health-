About Us

Who We Are

The FASD United Mental Health Work Group is a mental health collaborative dedicated to advancing brain-based, neurodevelopmentally informed care for individuals with FASD and other brain-based differences.

This website represents the collective expertise of:

  • Licensed mental health professionals specializing in FASD and neurodevelopmental conditions
  • FASD researchers and advocates
  • Members of the FASD United Mental Health Work Group
  • Caregivers and individuals with lived experience

All resources are reviewed by qualified professionals and regularly updated to reflect current evidence-based practices.

Our Approach

The Brain-Based Pathway is the central framework guiding our professional resources. It is a developmental roadmap for trauma-informed, neurobehavioral practice, supporting clinicians from foundational brain-based understanding to advanced, responsive care across six competency stages.

By integrating FASD, neurodevelopmental science, trauma, and neurodiversity-affirming practice, the pathway promotes individualized, compassionate, and effective mental health support for people whose brains work differently.

This site exists because we believe the shift to brain-based practice is worth making and that clinicians, caregivers, and self-advocates should not have to make it alone.

Accessibility and Inclusion

We are committed to providing accessible, inclusive resources for people of all neurotypes, abilities, ages, races, ethnicities, sexual orientations, gender identities, and socioeconomic backgrounds.

Accessibility Features

  • Plain language explanations of complex concepts
  • Multiple formats for learning (text, video, audio, and graphics when possible)
  • Neurodiversity-affirming, non-pathologizing language
  • Trauma-informed content design and attention to sensory load

Accessibility is an ongoing commitment. If you encounter barriers or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us using the information below.
