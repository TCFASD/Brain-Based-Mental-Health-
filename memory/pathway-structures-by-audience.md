The Brain-Based Pathway currently exists only as a clinician-specific developmental roadmap (six competency stages from foundational understanding to advanced, responsive practice). The site has three audiences but only one of them has a pathway. The strategic gap: caregivers and self-advocates land on the site without a parallel structured journey.

These are proposed structures for parallel pathways. Not yet implemented on the website. Tamra raised this on 2026-05-24 when working on the About Us > Our Approach section; she opted to use a "philosophy first, audience-specific later" framing in the meantime (see About Us mockup, Our Approach section).

**The Caregiver Pathway** — a developmental roadmap for supporting someone whose brain works differently.

1. Why This Matters — Why brain-based care changes outcomes for your family.
2. Understanding the Brain — How brains develop, regulate, and process information. What brain-based differences look like day to day.
3. Seeing the Brain in Your Child — Recognizing where your child's brain needs support and where behavior is intentional.
4. Asking for the Right Care — Knowing what to look for in providers, what to ask for, what does not fit.
5. Adapting What You Do — Parenting and support strategies that match how their brain works.
6. Finding Community — Connecting with other caregivers, advocates, and supports who understand.

**The Self-Advocate Pathway** — a developmental roadmap for understanding your own brain and asking for what you need.

1. Why This Matters — Why understanding your own brain matters for your own care.
2. Understanding the Brain — How brains differ, including yours.
3. Seeing Your Brain — Recognizing your strengths, your challenges, your patterns.
4. Naming What You Need — Language and tools for telling providers, partners, employers, and others what fits.
5. Adapting Your Environment — Building routines, supports, and accommodations that fit your brain.
6. Finding Community — Connecting with others who have similar brains and experiences.

**Parallels with the clinician pathway:** All three follow the same arc — Why → Understand → Recognize/Assess → Plan/Ask → Adapt → Connect. The clinician version is professional-development focused (competency stages); the caregiver and self-advocate versions are personally applicable (what you do day to day). Using the parallel structure as a unifying device on the website would let all three audiences feel like they are following the same kind of journey, just from their own angle.

**Why this matters strategically:** A unified pathway across audiences would let Our Approach on About Us introduce one framework that genuinely speaks to all three, instead of a clinician-specific framework with caregivers and self-advocates as add-ons. It would also give each audience page a clear progression structure rather than a flat list of resources.

**How to apply:** When the working group is ready to build out caregiver and self-advocate sections of the site, propose these pathway structures as the organizing skeleton. Refine the stage names and content together with caregivers and self-advocates (lived-experience input is essential, not optional, for these). Revisit the Our Approach section on About Us once the caregiver and self-advocate pathways exist.
