When writing any copy, page text, bios, or content the user will publish on the Brain-Based Mental Health website, avoid the patterns that flag prose as AI-generated.

Things to avoid: em-dashes (use commas or periods); the "It's not just X, it's Y" sentence pattern; words like "navigate," "leverage," "delve," "tapestry," "robust framework," "in today's landscape"; overly balanced parallel structures; thesaurus-polished sentences; "I'd be happy to" filler.

**Why:** The user explicitly flagged this when asking me to draft team and partnership sections. She wants the site to read as written by humans in the field, not by an AI.

**How to apply:** Apply to all website body copy, page descriptions, bios, and outreach text. Lower stakes in chat back-and-forth, but still keep prose plain. If something I draft reads machine-shaped, name it and offer to redo.
