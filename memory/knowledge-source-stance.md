A meaningful portion of the content on the Brain-Based Mental Health site comes from the real-world experience of clinicians doing this work, not from completed peer-reviewed research. The work group made an intentional choice to share what they know now rather than wait years for research to catch up. Research in the field is ongoing, and the site will be updated as new findings come in.

**Why:** People in the field (clinicians, caregivers, self-advocates) need guidance now. Waiting for the research to fully validate every practice would mean withholding help. The user wants visitors to understand this stance clearly so they can weigh the content honestly and trust the source.

**How to apply:** This stance should be visible on the site, not just internal context. Suggested placement:
- **Home:** A short band that names continued learning as part of how this field works, says some content here comes from practice experience, that research is developing, and that the site evolves as the field does. No CTA, just a stance.
- **About Us > Our Approach:** A fuller paragraph that names the mix of experience and research, and the commitment to update as findings emerge.
- **Clinicians > CoP page:** Picks the same value up as the clinician-specific expression (continued learning happens here together).

Frame it as a strength, not a caveat. The honesty builds trust.
