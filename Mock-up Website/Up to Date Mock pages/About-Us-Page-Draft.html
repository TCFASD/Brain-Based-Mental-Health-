About Us

Who We Are

The FASD United Mental Health Work Group is a mental health collaborative dedicated to advancing brain-based, neurodevelopmentally informed care for individuals with FASD and other brain-based differences.

Clinicians, caregivers, self-advocates, and researchers recognized a gap in how individuals with brain-based differences are understood. We came together as a community to close that gap through education and training, and to make brain-based care more accessible to those who need it.

All resources are reviewed by clinicians and researchers with FASD expertise and regularly updated to reflect current evidence, organized through the Brain-Based Pathway framework.

Our Approach

Our approach starts with a simple idea: support should match how the brain actually works. We begin with understanding the brain itself, whether the difference is developmental, acquired, or trauma-related, before choosing how to help.

What this looks like depends on who you are. Clinicians follow a developmental pathway from foundational understanding to advanced, responsive practice. Caregivers learn to recognize what they are seeing in their child and how to find care that fits. Self-advocates build understanding of their own brain and the language to ask for what they need.

This site exists because we believe the shift to brain-based care is worth making, and that clinicians, caregivers, and self-advocates shouldn't have to make it alone.

Accessibility and Inclusion

We are committed to providing accessible, inclusive resources for people of all neurotypes, abilities, ages, races, ethnicities, sexual orientations, gender identities, and socioeconomic backgrounds.

Accessibility Features

  • Plain language explanations of complex concepts
  • Multiple formats for learning (text, video, audio, and graphics when possible)
  • Neurodiversity-affirming, non-pathologizing language
  • Trauma-informed content design and attention to sensory load

Accessibility is an ongoing commitment. If you encounter barriers or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us.
