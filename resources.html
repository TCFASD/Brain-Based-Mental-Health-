Informed Approaches

These organizations and programs offer evidence-based, brain-informed frameworks for working with FASD and neurodevelopmental differences.

1

FASCETS

The Family Survival and Community Empowerment Through Science program uses the Neurobehavioral Model to help families, professionals, and communities understand brain-based differences and create better-fitting environments.

Visit FASCETS

2

Families Moving Forward

An evidence-based program that supports caregivers of children with FASD and other neurodevelopmental conditions through structured, brain-based guidance and skill-building.

Visit Families Moving Forward

3

Think Brain First

A framework developed by Dr. Eileen Devine that helps caregivers and professionals understand neurodevelopmental differences and respond with strategies that match brain functioning.

Visit Think Brain First

4

THO - Towards Healthy Outcomes

A comprehensive resource that provides evidence-informed strategies for supporting individuals with FASD across the lifespan.

5

IMPACT

A collaborative project focused on improving outcomes for individuals and families affected by FASD through research, education, and clinical resources.

FASD United's Language and Stigma Guide

This resource helps professionals, educators, and caregivers identify stigmatizing language and use respectful, person-first messaging when discussing FASD, substance use, and lived experience.
